Featured Projects

Racial Disparities in Jury Selection

Applied doubly robust causal inference methods to analyze prosecutorial peremptory strikes on 2,300 jurors with 120 covariates from Mississippi courts (1992-2017).

Key findings: Identified 37 percentage point difference in strike rates between Black and white jurors using variable importance measures and sensitivity analysis.

Presented at Joint Statistical Meetings 2025, Nashville, TN

Gender Disparity Analysis in Social Media (Reddit)

Established three-stage framework combining text processing, matching methods, and causal estimation to assess gender disparities on r/relationships using 97,000 Reddit posts.

Methods: Implemented structural topic modeling, sentiment analysis, propensity score matching, and cardinality matching to control for writing style and topic confounders.

Presented at American Causal Inference Conference 2023, Austin, TX

Algorithmic Fairness in Housing Assessment

Evaluated fairness and effectiveness of Allegheny Housing Assessment (AHA) algorithm using 25,000 records from 2018-2023.

Analysis: Assessed three-LASSO prediction model for adverse outcomes (mental health, ER visits, jail bookings) across racial groups and compared disparate impact with traditional VI-SPDAT survey.
